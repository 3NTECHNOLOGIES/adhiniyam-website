Documents Required From New Employees

A practical HR joining checklist for employers, HR teams and payroll users in India.

Quick Answer

Common documents required from new employees may include PAN card, Aadhaar card, address proof, passport-size photograph, bank details, educational certificates, previous employment documents, UAN/PF details, ESIC details where applicable and signed joining forms or declarations.

Why Employee Documents Are Important

Employee documents help employers maintain proper identity, payroll, tax, PF/ESIC and HR records. They also support salary processing, salary slip preparation, background verification and future employment references.

Identity and Address Documents

  • PAN card for tax and salary records
  • Aadhaar card for identity/KYC records where applicable
  • Current and permanent address proof
  • Passport-size photograph or digital photograph

Bank and Payroll Details

Employers usually collect bank name, account holder name, account number, IFSC, branch details and cancelled cheque or passbook copy where required. Correct bank details help avoid salary payment delays.

Education and Experience Documents

Educational certificates, mark sheets, experience letter, relieving letter, previous appointment letter and last salary slips may be collected depending on role and company policy.

PF, UAN and ESIC Details

If PF or ESIC applies, HR may collect UAN, previous PF member details, ESIC insurance number, nominee details and family details where required. Existing UAN or ESIC numbers should be shared to avoid duplicate records.

Joining Forms and Declarations

A joining form may collect personal details, nominee details, emergency contact, education, experience and bank details. Employers may also collect confidentiality, code of conduct, document authenticity and policy acknowledgement declarations.

Employee Joining Checklist

  • PAN and Aadhaar
  • Address proof and photograph
  • Bank details
  • Education and experience documents
  • UAN / ESIC details where applicable
  • Emergency contact and nominee details
  • Signed offer or appointment letter
  • Joining form and declarations

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Collecting incomplete bank or tax details
  • Ignoring name mismatches across PAN, Aadhaar and bank account
  • Creating duplicate UAN records
  • Not keeping signed employee acceptance
  • Asking for unnecessary sensitive documents

Practical Example

A small company hiring an Accounts Executive may collect PAN, Aadhaar, bank details, address proof, education documents, previous employment documents, UAN details, emergency contact and a signed appointment letter before processing payroll.
