Trademark Registration vs Company Name Registration

Understand why company name approval and trademark registration are different brand protection steps.

6 min readRelated Tool: Business ToolsRelated Service: Trademark Registration
Quick Answer

Company name registration creates or identifies a legal business entity. Trademark registration helps protect a brand name, logo, product name or service identity. Registering a company name does not automatically give full trademark protection over the brand name.

What is Company Name Registration?

Company name registration is part of incorporating a company or LLP. It gives the business a legal name used for MCA records, bank account, contracts, tax registrations and official communication.

What is Trademark Registration?

Trademark registration is a separate process for protecting a brand identity such as a brand name, logo, product name, service name, slogan or label.

Main Difference

Company name identifies the legal entity. Trademark protects the brand identity, subject to trademark law, class selection, examination and registration process.

Why Company Registration Is Not Enough

A company can have one legal name and many brand names, product names or service names. Brand protection should be planned separately.

Can Company Name Be Trademarked?

Yes, a company name or part of it may be filed as a trademark if it is used as a brand and is eligible for trademark protection.

Startup and Small Business Approach

Businesses should check MCA name availability, trademark availability, domain availability and social handles before investing heavily in branding.

Company Name vs Trade Name vs Trademark

  • Company name: legal entity name
  • Trade name: operating name
  • Brand name: market-facing identity
  • Trademark: legal protection for brand identity, subject to law

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Assuming company approval means trademark protection
  • Not doing trademark search
  • Filing wrong class
  • Ignoring logo vs wordmark difference
  • Waiting too long before filing

Practical Example

A company may be registered as Brightwave Technologies Private Limited but sell software under the brand name LedgerPilot. If it wants to protect LedgerPilot, trademark filing should be considered separately.
