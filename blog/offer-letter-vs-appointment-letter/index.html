Offer Letter vs Appointment Letter

Understand the practical difference between offer letters and appointment letters in HR documentation for employers and employees.

5 min readRelated Tool: Salary Slip GeneratorRelated Service: Payroll Support
Quick Answer

An offer letter is generally issued to a selected candidate before joining to communicate the job offer, role, salary and joining details. An appointment letter is generally issued after acceptance or at the time of joining and contains detailed employment terms such as designation, salary structure, probation, notice period, workplace, duties, policies and other conditions.

What is an Offer Letter?

An offer letter is a formal communication from an employer to a selected candidate. It usually includes designation, joining date, work location, salary or CTC summary, acceptance deadline and basic conditions.

What is an Appointment Letter?

An appointment letter is a more detailed employment document confirming the appointment and employment terms. It may include salary structure, probation, notice period, working hours, duties, policies, confidentiality and acceptance section.

Main Difference Between Offer Letter and Appointment Letter

  • Purpose: Offer letter communicates the offer; appointment letter confirms employment terms.
  • Stage: Offer letter is usually before joining; appointment letter is issued after acceptance or during joining.
  • Detail level: Offer letter is usually shorter; appointment letter is more detailed.

When is an Offer Letter Issued?

It is usually issued after interview selection, role finalization, salary discussion and internal approval, before the candidate joins.

When is an Appointment Letter Issued?

It is usually issued after the candidate accepts the offer, on or before joining, or during onboarding as part of employment documentation.

Common Employer Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not issuing written documents
  • Missing salary or joining details
  • Unclear probation or notice period
  • Not mentioning work location
  • Not keeping signed acceptance
  • Copy-pasting outdated formats

Offer Letter vs Appointment Letter vs Joining Letter

An offer letter is issued by the employer before joining. An appointment letter confirms employment terms. A joining letter is usually submitted by the employee to confirm joining duty.

Practical Example

A company selects a candidate for Accounts Executive. First it issues an offer letter with designation, joining date and salary summary. After acceptance and joining, it issues an appointment letter with final salary structure, probation, notice period and policy terms.
