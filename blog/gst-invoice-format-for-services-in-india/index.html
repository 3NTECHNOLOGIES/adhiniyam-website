GST Invoice Format for Services in India

A practical guide for freelancers, consultants, agencies and service providers preparing GST invoices for services in India.

Quick Answer

A GST invoice for services should generally include supplier details, GSTIN, invoice number, invoice date, customer details, place of supply, service description, SAC code where applicable, taxable value, GST rate, CGST/SGST or IGST breakup, total invoice value, payment details and relevant notes.

What is a GST Service Invoice?

A GST service invoice is a tax invoice issued by a GST-registered service provider for taxable services. It records who provided the service, who received it, the value of service, applicable GST rate and the final amount payable.

Who May Need a GST Invoice for Services?

  • Freelancers and consultants
  • Digital marketing and creative agencies
  • Software and website developers
  • Accountants, designers and service startups
  • Training, coaching and professional service providers

Important Fields in a GST Service Invoice

  • Supplier name, address and GSTIN
  • Invoice number and invoice date
  • Customer name, address and GSTIN where applicable
  • Place of supply
  • Description of services
  • SAC code where applicable
  • Taxable value, GST rate and GST amount
  • CGST/SGST or IGST breakup
  • Payment details and terms

CGST + SGST or IGST in Service Invoices

GST breakup depends on place of supply. In many simple cases, same-state supplies show CGST and SGST, while different-state supplies show IGST. The correct treatment should always be verified based on the nature of service and applicable GST rules.

Sample GST Service Invoice Format

A basic service invoice may include supplier details, customer details, invoice number/date, service description, SAC code, taxable value, GST rate, tax breakup, total value, payment details and a system-generated note.

Common Mistakes in Service Invoices

  • Missing SAC code where applicable
  • Wrong GST type or place of supply
  • Incorrect GSTIN
  • Duplicate invoice number
  • Unclear service description
  • Wrong GST rate

Practical Example

A website developer registered under GST provides services worth ₹10,000 to a client in another state. The invoice should show service description, taxable value, applicable GST rate, IGST amount and final total, along with payment details.
